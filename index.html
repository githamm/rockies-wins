Colorado Rockies vs National League West

Since 1993, team has worst winning percentage among rivals

In the 23 seasons since the Colorado Rockies joined the league in 1993, the team has posted seven winning seasons. Against National League West opponents from 1993-2015, the Rockies have a winning record against only the San Diego Padres.

How the chart works
Bars between teams are colored by the team with a winning record against the other team (ex: bar between Rockies and Padres is purple because the Rockies have a winning record against the Padres); bar ends are proportionate to wins. Hover over team name to highlight bars; hover over team name and bars for details.

The Rockies' record against NL West teams, in order of winning percentage:

  • San Diego Padres, 195-183 (.516)
  • San Francisco Giants, 166-210 (.442)
  • Arizona Diamondbacks, 135-181 (.427)
  • Los Angeles Dodgers, 161-217 (.426)

The Los Angeles Dodgers and San Francisco Giants have been the bullies on the block, posting a cumulative winning record against all NL West opponents from 1993-2015. The San Diego Padres have a losing record against other NL West teams, but have a higher winning percentage than the Rockies.

Total team wins since 1993, in order of winning percentage:

  • Dodgers, 782-663 (.541)
  • Giants, 774-666 (.538)
  • Diamondbacks, 625-638 (.495)*
  • Padres, 681-761 (.472)
  • Rockies, 657-791 (.454)

* The Diamondbacks entered the league in 1998
